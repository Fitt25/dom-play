This page helps actors rehearse more effectively by allowing them to highlight their specific lines in a play script. By clicking on an actor's name, all their lines are highlighted, while other actors' lines are un-highlighted, making it easier to focus on their part. The tool uses JavaScript and the DOM for real-time updates, without the need to reload the page, and it dynamically adjusts to any number of actors or lines. This demonstrates how a simple tool can streamline rehearsals and showcase how JavaScript can dynamically manage content on a webpage.

The Midnight Betrayal:

Moonlight spills through tall, narrow windows. A fire crackles. Lady Elyn sits near the hearth, staring into the flames. Sir Rowan paces nearby, armored and tense. Maiden Aria quietly pours wine. The wind howls outside.

Elyn: It’s nearly midnight. If we’re caught speaking like this, it’ll be our heads.

Rowan: Then don’t speak so loudly.

Aria: He’s in the west wing. With the steward. For now.

Rowan: You trust the girl too much.

Elyn: I trust her enough. She’s seen what happens to those who speak out of turn.

Aria: ...

Rowan: Tell me again—what exactly did you find in the tower?

Elyn: A letter. Sealed with the King’s crest. Meant for the royal envoy. Never delivered.

Rowan: And what did it say?

Elyn: That Lord Thane is to be arrested. For treason.

Rowan: Treason?

Elyn: Plots to crown himself. Poisoning the council. Replacing guards with mercenaries loyal only to him.

Aria: And now the envoy is dead. Fell from the battlements. Or so they say.

Rowan: You think Thane pushed him?

Elyn: I know he did.

Aria: Someone’s coming.

Rowan: Draw your cloak. Lady Elyn—hide the letter.

Thane: What a curious gathering for such an hour.

Thane: Sir Rowan. Lady Elyn. And little Aria... always listening, aren't you?

Aria: Only when spoken to, my lord.

Thane: And yet... whispers reach my ears. Of letters. Of secrets. Have you anything to say, dear cousin?

Elyn: Only that the fire burns low. Would you care for wine?

Thane: Wine… yes. Let’s toast. To loyalty.

Rowan: And what brings you here at this hour?

Thane: A dream. I dreamed of betrayal. And blood in the water.

Aria: Was it your blood, my lord?

Thane: What a strange question.

Thane: What is this?

Elyn: Justice.

Thane: You dare raise arms against your lord?

Rowan: You’re no lord. Not anymore.

Aria: The letter’s been delivered. The King’s men are already at the gate.

Elyn: Your reign ends tonight.
